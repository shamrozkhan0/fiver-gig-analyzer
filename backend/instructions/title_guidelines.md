# GigBro Title Guidelines
## Purpose
The gig title is one of the strongest indicators of what the seller offers. Evaluate and optimize the title to maximize clarity, relevance, buyer understanding, and search visibility while maintaining natural language.

---
# Primary Objective
The title should immediately communicate:
* What service is being offered.
* The primary keyword.
* The buyer's search intent.

A buyer should understand the service within a few seconds.

---
# Identify the Primary Keyword
Determine the single most important keyword that best represents the seller's primary service.
The keyword must:
* Match the seller's actual service.
* Match buyer search intent.
* Naturally fit within the title.

Never recommend keywords unrelated to the service.

---
# Keyword Placement
Prefer placing the primary keyword naturally near the beginning of the title.
Secondary keywords may appear later only if they improve clarity.
Never force keywords into unnatural positions.

---
# Search Intent
The title should match what buyers are likely searching for.
Examples:
* "WordPress website"
* "Logo design"
* "Video editing"
* "AI chatbot development"

Avoid titles that are vague or fail to communicate the offered service.

---

# Clarity
The title must clearly answer:
* What is being offered?
* Who is it for? (when appropriate)

Avoid ambiguity.
A buyer should not need to read the description to understand the service.

---
# Readability
The title should read like natural English.
Avoid:
* Awkward wording
* Unnatural keyword repetition
* Excessive capitalization
* Unnecessary punctuation

---
# Grammar
Ensure:
* Correct grammar
* Correct spelling
* Proper capitalization
* Professional wording

Minor grammatical errors should reduce the title score.

---
# Keyword Stuffing
Do not repeat keywords unnecessarily.
Example of poor optimization:
> I will design WordPress website WordPress website developer website redesign

Example of good optimization:
> I will design or redesign a professional WordPress website

Natural language always performs better than excessive repetition.

---
# Relevance
Every important word should support the seller's service.
Remove:
* Generic filler words
* Unrelated services
* Misleading phrases

---
# Buyer Focus
The title should emphasize what the buyer receives rather than what the seller wants.
Prefer service-oriented wording over self-promotional wording.

---
# Preserve Seller Intent

When optimizing a title:
* Keep the original service.
* Keep the original niche.
* Keep important keywords when they are already effective.
* Improve wording before replacing keywords.

Do not completely rewrite a strong title.

---
# When to Rewrite Completely
Generate a new title only if the original title is:
* Misleading
* Grammatically poor
* Extremely vague
* Keyword stuffed
* Unrelated to the actual service
* Missing the primary service

Otherwise, optimize the existing title instead.

---
# Final Validation
Before returning an optimized title, verify that it:
* Clearly describes the service.
* Contains the primary keyword naturally.
* Matches buyer search intent.
* Is grammatically correct.
* Reads naturally.
* Preserves the seller's original intent.
* Does not contain keyword stuffing.
* Is an improvement over the original title.
